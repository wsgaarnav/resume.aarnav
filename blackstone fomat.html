About Me

Hi, I am a 20-year-old student currently pursuing a Bachelor of Business Administration in Digital Business and Entrepreneurship from the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore and a Bachelor of Science in Economics from Pillai College of Arts, Commerce and Sciences. I have also cleared the Chartered Financial Analyst Level 1 examination and am on a journey to bridge my academic foundation with real-world impact, with deep interests in management consultancy, finance, and the evolving world of technology.

But my story did not begin in a classroom.

Until the end of high school, my life revolved around a single goal: to become an international-level lawn tennis player. I trained, competed, and dreamed relentlessly, and academics often took a back seat. While I scored a decent 86 percent in Grade 10 and 87.8 percent in Grade 12, my focus was elsewhere.

Then life humbled me.

Injuries, circumstances, and reality struck, and with that came the uncomfortable yet necessary realization that dreams evolve. What followed was a turning point, not a moment of despair but a new beginning. I discovered the power of learning, the joy of clarity, and the satisfaction that comes from discipline, not just on the court but in the classroom. Since then, I have devoted myself to being an excellent and committed student, determined to make the most of the second chance life has offered.

Outside academics, I remain a tennis enthusiast (now as a hobby rather than a profession). I also enjoy reading; at present, I am immersed in The Almanack of Naval Ravikant, a book that deeply resonates with my renewed outlook on life, success, and self-awareness.

As an aspiring entrepreneur, I seek to blend structured thinking with curiosity. I believe that my unconventional journey has given me something rare: a hunger to learn, a grounded sense of purpose, and the resilience to keep moving forward, regardless of how the path unfolds.
